Early Life

In his childhood he spent six years as an Eagle Scout, making him one of the few men who can boast of that distinction. His older brother was also an Eagle Scout but then died in a tragic boating accident on the Potomac River when George was still eight years old. He attended Woodrow Wilson High School where he excelled in football, baseball, basketball, archery, fencing, marching band, fencing marching band, regular band, theater, film, painting, graphic design, 2d animation, 3d animation, choir, show choir, show and tell choir, Magic the Gathering, stage magic, literal magic and track. He set national records at discus, javelin, shot put, high jump, long jump, 100-meter dash, 200-meter dash, 400-meter relay, 800-meter relay, mile run, mile run relay, mile and a half relay, 110-meter hurdles, 300-meter hurdle relay, and wizard dueling. He won state titles in all of them except for the triple jump. At the end of his senior year, George was named "All State" in everything. There were no national championships in any sport back then, however, so George did not receive a gold medal or trophy or anything because he turned them down every time. in his home life, George was raised by two loving parents and no sibling (not after the accident). His father was a computer programmer and his mother an accountant. When he was four, George's father bought him his first computer, and from that point forward he became a master hacker. It soon became clear that this early interest in computers would eventually take over his life. By the age of seven, he had already made himself one of the best programmers in the world. His work has been used by police agencies around the globe and by numerous government departments in Washington. He soon quit because government work is boring.

Career

In his life he has had many careers, all of them in fact. He was an actor and a writer, a painter and sculptor, a dancer, a musician, a designer and an architect, a scholar and a teacher, a student and a doctor. He has been a spy, a soldier, a sailor, a pilot, an astronaut, a sorcerer, a wizard, a warlock, a druid, a scientist, an inventor, a philosopher, a mathematician, a lawyer, a salesman, a poet, a playwright, a historian, a mechanic, and a chef. George is currently a professor of engineering at Harvard University, where he teaches courses in robotics, artificial intelligence, and pastries. One of his students is working on creating a machine that will be able to think like George but every time it tries the power goes out and the computer explodes. George, of course, is very upset about this. Despite his many accomplishments, George is not arrogant. He does not believe he is better than anybody else and never has. In fact, George believes everyone else is far luckier than him, as he will never get an opportunity to meet George. If he did, that would mean someone else had met George and then George would know they had and then George would realize he had not met George so then he would feel bad and go home and lie down. George's greatest accomplishment was learning how to walk backwards while juggling three bowling pins. This feat was done by following the instructions in Chapter Three of his autobiography, "How I Learned to Walk Backwards While Juggling Bowling Pins," which was published in 1982. The book was printed in a limited edition of 500 copies.

Future

In the future he plans to create a robot that can make hot cocoa without spilling any. Because that is something he wants. Also, George wishes to build a house with a cupola. And a moat. Just a normal moat. Not a magical one. He already did that. He also has plans to start a family. He is hoping to find a girl who can sing and draw and dance and play guitar and be funny. He also plans to branch out into charity work as he has already become super duper insanely rich. He hopes to do this by collecting enough money to buy a house big enough to fit all of the homeless people in it. Then he can feed them and keep them warm and he won't have to worry about them anymore.

Other projects he is planning include:

  • Becoming President of the United States.
  • Learning to cook again.
  • Getting a dog.
  • Starting a revolution. (Probably.)
  • Writing a sequel to How I Learned to Walk Backwards While Juggling Bowling Pins.
  • And finally, his biggest dream is to someday sit next to George on an airplane and not have to get up when he gets off.
